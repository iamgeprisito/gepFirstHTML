Chicken and Pork Adobo

Chicken and Pork Adobo is a version of Filipino adobo using the combination of chicken and pork meats. Adobo is considered as the signature dish of the Philippines. Almost all meat, seafood, and vegetables can be cooked using the inadobo method. This is best served with steamed rice.

Adobo, in general, can be cooked using many different types of protein. Chicken or pork are commonly used ingredients. Have you tried cooking Filipino chicken and pork Adobo? I hope this recipe can help you. The word Adobo comes from the Spanish word "adobar". It means "to marinate". This can be in the form of a liquid bath or by rubbing using a combination of powdered ingredients. This Filipino version of Adobo suggests marinating the meat with soy sauce and crushed garlic. Preferably, vinegar can also be added as a marinade.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly
  • 2 lbs chicken thighs
  • 3 tbsp crushed or minced garlic
  • 6 pieces dired bay leaves
  • 8 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1 1/2 tbsp peppercorn
  • 4 cups water
  • salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Combine the pork belly and chicked, soy sauce, and garlic then marinade for at least 1 hour
  2. Heat the pot and put-in the marinated pork belly then cook for a few minutes
  3. Pour remaining marinade including garlic.
  4. Add water, whole pepper corn, and dried bay leaves then bring to a boil. Simmer for 40 minutes to 1 hour
  5. Put-in the vinegar and simmer for 12 to 15 minutes
  6. Add salt to taste.
  7. Serve hot on steamed rice. Share and enjoy!
